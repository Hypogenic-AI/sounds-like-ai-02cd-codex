# Downloaded Papers

1. **Steering Llama 2 via Contrastive Activation Addition** (Panickssery et al., 2024)
   - File: `papers/2312.06681_contrastive_activation_addition.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2312.06681
   - Why relevant: Canonical residual-stream steering method (CAA) that defines linear directions for behaviors.

2. **Selective Steering: Norm-Preserving Control Through Discriminative Layer Selection** (Dang & Ngo, 2026)
   - File: `papers/2601.19375_selective_steering.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2601.19375
   - Why relevant: Norm-preserving, layer-selective steering; important for reliable residual-stream interventions.

3. **Improved Representation Steering for Language Models** (Wu et al., 2025)
   - File: `papers/2505.20809_representation_projection_steering.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2505.20809
   - Why relevant: RePS objective for steering/suppression; evaluates steering vs prompting on AXBENCH.

4. **Steering Vector Fields for Context-Aware Inference-Time Control in Large Language Models** (Li et al., 2026)
   - File: `papers/2602.01654_steering_vector_fields.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2602.01654
   - Why relevant: Context-dependent vector-field steering (addresses brittleness of global vectors).

5. **On the Identifiability of Steering Vectors in Large Language Models** (Venkatesh & Kurapath, 2026)
   - File: `papers/2602.06801_identifiability_of_steering_vectors.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2602.06801
   - Why relevant: Formal limits on interpretability/uniqueness of steering directions.

6. **Endogenous Resistance to Activation Steering in Language Models** (McKenzie et al., 2026)
   - File: `papers/2602.06941_endogenous_resistance_activation_steering.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2602.06941
   - Why relevant: Evidence of model self-correction against misaligned steering; important for robustness.

7. **Interpretable LLM Guardrails via Sparse Representation Steering** (He et al., 2025)
   - File: `papers/2503.16851_sparse_representation_steering.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2503.16851
   - Why relevant: SAE-based sparse steering for controllability and multi-attribute control.

8. **Residual Stream Analysis with Multi-Layer SAEs** (Lawson et al., 2025, ICLR)
   - File: `papers/2409.04185_residual_stream_analysis_multi_layer_saes.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2409.04185
   - Why relevant: Multi-layer SAE analysis of residual stream; useful for discovering style directions.

9. **HC3: Human ChatGPT Comparison Corpus** (Guo et al., 2023)
   - File: `papers/2301.07597_hc3_human_chatgpt_comparison_corpus.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2301.07597
   - Why relevant: Benchmark dataset for human vs AI text detection (proxy for “sounds like AI”).

10. **HC3 Plus: A Semantic-Invariant Human ChatGPT Comparison Corpus** (Su et al., 2024)
    - File: `papers/2309.02731_hc3_plus.pdf`
    - arXiv: 2309.02731
    - Why relevant: Adds semantic-invariant tasks (summarization/translation/paraphrase) for AI text detection.
